TIMEX

Timex, established in 1854 as the Waterbury Clock Company in Waterbury, Connecticut, has a rich heritage in watchmaking. The company was a pioneer in producing affordable timepieces, making quality watches accessible to the general public.

Historical Milestones

  • 1854: Founded as the Waterbury Clock Company, focusing on mass-producing affordable timepieces.
  • 1941: Thomas Olsen acquired the company, renaming it Timex Corporation. The name "Timex" was inspired by combining the names of popular magazines of the time, "Time" and "Kleenex."
  • 1950: Introduced watches under the Timex brand, becoming the most affordable watches on the American market.
  • 1960s: Gained popularity with the slogan "Takes a licking and keeps on ticking," emphasizing the durability of their watches.
  • 1980s: Expanded into digital watches and introduced the Ironman Triathlon series, catering to sports enthusiasts.
  • 1992: Launched the Indiglo night-light feature, enhancing watch readability in low-light conditions.

Product Lines and Notable Models

  • Timex Weekender: A versatile and casual watch known for its minimalist design and interchangeable straps. Approximate price: $30-$40.
  • Timex Expedition Scout: Designed for outdoor enthusiasts, featuring a rugged build and Indiglo backlight. Approximate price: $40-$50.
  • Timex Ironman Classic 30: A digital sports watch with a 30-lap memory, countdown timer, and water resistance up to 100 meters. Approximate price: $50-$60.
  • Timex Marlin Automatic: A reissue of a 1960s classic, featuring a sleek design with an automatic movement. Approximate price: $250-$270.
  • Q Timex Reissue: A revival of the 1970s quartz watch, featuring a retro design with modern updates. Approximate price: $180-$200.

Innovation and Technology

Timex has been at the forefront of watch innovation, introducing features like the Indiglo night-light and durable designs that cater to both casual wearers and athletes. Their commitment to affordability without compromising quality has made them a staple in the watch industry.

Global Presence

With a strong international presence, Timex watches are available worldwide through various retailers and online platforms. The brand's dedication to quality and innovation continues to resonate with consumers globally.
