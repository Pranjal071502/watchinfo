Casio

Casio, established in 1946 by Tadao Kashio in Mitaka, Tokyo, Japan, is a renowned multinational electronics company celebrated for its wide array of products, including calculators, musical instruments, and notably, watches.

Historical Milestones

  • 1946: Tadao Kashio founded Kashio Seisakujo in Mitaka, Tokyo.
  • 1974: Casio introduced the Casiotron, one of the world's first digital wristwatches featuring an automatic calendar, marking the company's entry into the watch market.
  • 1983: The G-Shock series was launched, starting with the DW-5000C, known for its durability and resistance to shocks.
  • 1994: The Baby-G series was introduced, offering a durable and stylish watch line targeted towards women.

Innovation and Technology

Casio has been at the forefront of integrating technology into timepieces, offering features such as solar power, atomic timekeeping, and robust shock-resistant designs. The G-Shock series, in particular, is celebrated for its resilience and multifunctionality, appealing to both adventurers and urban dwellers.

Global Presence

With a strong international presence, Casio's watches are available worldwide through various retailers and online platforms. The brand's commitment to quality, innovation, and affordability has solidified its position as a leader in the watch industry.
