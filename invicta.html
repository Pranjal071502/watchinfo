Invicta

Invicta, established in 1837 by Raphael Picard in La Chaux-de-Fonds, Switzerland, is a watch company renowned for offering high-quality timepieces at accessible prices.

Company Overview

The name "Invicta," meaning "invincible" in Latin, reflects the brand's commitment to durability and excellence. Initially, Invicta focused on crafting mechanical and automatic watches, emphasizing Swiss craftsmanship. However, during the quartz crisis of the 1970s, the company faced challenges that led to a decline. In 1991, Invicta was revitalized by new ownership, relocating its headquarters to Hollywood, Florida, USA. This resurgence allowed the brand to blend traditional watchmaking techniques with modern innovations, expanding its product line to include quartz and automatic movements.

Innovation and Craftsmanship

Invicta is known for its commitment to quality and innovation, offering a wide range of designs from classic to contemporary. The brand utilizes various materials and movements, including both quartz and automatic, to cater to diverse customer preferences.

Global Presence

With a strong international presence, Invicta continues to expand its market reach, offering timepieces that combine style, functionality, and affordability.
