Festina

Festina, established in 1902 in La Chaux-de-Fonds, Switzerland, by the Stüdi brothers, has a rich heritage in watchmaking. The brand's name, "Festina," is derived from the Latin phrase "Festina Lente," meaning "make haste slowly," reflecting a commitment to precision and thoughtful craftsmanship.

Historical Milestones

  • 1935: Businessman Willy Burkhard von Wilhelm acquired Festina, relocating the brand to Barcelona, Spain, with support from Adolf Hoffmann during World War II.
  • 1984: Spanish entrepreneur Miguel Rodríguez, founder of the Lotus watch brand, purchased Festina, leading to the formation of Festina Lotus S.A. This strategic acquisition expanded the company's market presence and diversified its product offerings.
  • 1989: The company acquired the Jaguar watch brand, further enhancing its portfolio.
  • 1990: Festina reached a production milestone of 1 million watches per year, increasing to 3 million by 2002, indicating significant growth and market penetration.

Innovation and Technology

Festina is renowned for its blend of traditional craftsmanship and modern technology. The brand emphasizes quality materials, precise movements, and innovative designs, appealing to a broad audience. Their collections often feature durable materials like titanium and incorporate functionalities suited for sports enthusiasts, such as chronographs and water resistance.

Global Presence

With a strong international presence, Festina's watches are available worldwide through various retailers and online platforms. The brand's commitment to quality and design has solidified its position in the global watch industry.
