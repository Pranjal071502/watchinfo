Seiko

Seiko, founded in 1881 by Kintaro Hattori in Tokyo, Japan, is a distinguished watch manufacturer renowned for its innovation and craftsmanship. The company began as a shop selling and repairing watches and clocks and has since evolved into a fully integrated watch manufacturer, designing and developing its own movements using leading-edge technology.

Historical Milestones

  • 1892: Established the 'Seikosha' factory, marking the beginning of Seiko's manufacturing journey.
  • 1913: Introduced Japan's first wristwatch, the Laurel.
  • 1924: Launched the first watch under the Seiko brand name.
  • 1969: Released the Seiko Quartz Astron, the world's first quartz wristwatch, revolutionizing the watch industry.
  • 1977: Introduced a solar-powered watch, showcasing Seiko's commitment to sustainable technology.

Innovation and Technology

Seiko is renowned for several technological advancements:

  • Quartz Movement: Pioneered the development of quartz watches, significantly enhancing accuracy.
  • Spring Drive: Introduced a unique movement combining the high torque of a mechanical watch with the accuracy of quartz.
  • Kinetic Technology: Developed watches powered by the movement of the wearer, eliminating the need for battery replacements.

Global Presence

Seiko has a significant global presence, offering a wide range of watches through its retail stores, authorized dealers, and online platforms. The brand's commitment to quality and innovation has established it as a leader in the watch industry.
